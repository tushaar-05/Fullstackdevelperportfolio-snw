My programming origins

When I was in elementary school, I enjoyed playing an old online multiplayer game called MapleStory. I thought MapleStory was cool! I thought: "Surely I could make this!" I was...mistaken.

But I enjoyed learning anyway. I spent hours and hours experimenting with C++, tinkering around with SDL and learning the hard way that programming isn't as easy as it looks.

Finding My Way to Web

Elsewhere I was in a completely different world. I was working with big data, SQL, ETL, etc. As time went on, I realized that this was not for me and my passions - although I still had love for working with data.

I was ready for a change, but I didn't know exactly what. I was terribly stuck. I was terribly stuck until one fateful day when someone told me to try front-end engineering.

Life Beyond Code

When I'm not busy behind a computer, I like to remain active. After coding sessions I like this mix. After exploring code, I swap debugging for bike riding, or tossing an oblong brown leather ball to my son. LEGO blocks and build projects let me scratch that architectural itch. It's a perfect blend. Work, sweat, family: equally loved parts of my life.

Music has also been big: whether it's songs or my family (couple guitars and keyboards at the ready) it's been and will be one of those huge parts of my life.

These Days

Currently, my journey takes all of my abilities as a senior software engineer and entrepreneurial-minded thinking to the work I do now. I balance it all with my remote jobs for various companies.

When I'm not working, you'll find me on social media using X. Speaking to other dev, reading about new frameworks, finding new ways of working creatively and adding to my personal brand. I want to remain flexible with my family, equally double-dip in my tech career for the coming years.

I'm Braydon - a senior front-end developer, blogger and public speaker. Thanks for checking out my site!
